Working Papers

Pollution Without Borders: Transboundary Air Pollution and the Geography of Pollutant Control Policy JOB MARKET PAPER
[Paper]
This paper investigates the spatial spillover effects of air pollution and how transboundary pollution shapes the geography of pollutant control policy. Using micro-level data and a structural spatial model, I study how pollution crosses administrative boundaries and how this externality affects the design and effectiveness of environmental regulation. The analysis reveals that ignoring transboundary pollution leads to significant welfare losses and suboptimal policy outcomes.

Gendered Labour Market Sorting in Response to Trade Shocks
This paper studies how trade shocks affect the sorting of men and women across occupations and industries in the labour market. Using detailed micro-level data, I document systematic differences in how male and female workers respond to import competition, with implications for the gender wage gap and labour market inequality.

Automation and the British Labour Market
with Rui Costa
We study the impact of automation technologies on the British labour market. Contrary to widespread fears about job losses, our research shows that the real concern is Britain's lack of productivity-enhancing robots, software, and artificial intelligence adoption. We analyse the distributional consequences of automation across regions and worker groups.
